task3

F1=2⋅(Precision⋅Recall)​ / (Precision+Recall)

Precision → “насколько ты точно находишь мошенников”
Recall → “насколько много мошенников ты находишь”

AUC-ROC показывает, насколько хорошо модель отличает fraud от normal


F1-score is a combination of precision and recall, which makes it suitable for imbalanced datasets like fraud detection.

Precision measures how many detected fraud transactions are actually fraud
Recall measures how many real fraud cases the model successfully detects
 Why not only accuracy?

Accuracy is not suitable because fraud cases are very rare.
For example, if 99% of transactions are normal, the model can achieve 99% accuracy by always predicting “not fraud,” which is useless.

 Trade-off explanation
High precision → fewer false alarms but may miss real fraud
High recall → catches more fraud but may flag normal users

F1-score balances both, ensuring that the model is both accurate and effective.


task4

 What could go wrong
False positives (normal users flagged as fraud)
Legitimate users may have their transactions blocked, causing frustration and loss of trust.
False negatives (fraud not detected)
The system may fail to detect some fraudulent transactions, leading to financial losses.
Class imbalance problem
Since fraud cases are rare, the model may learn to ignore them and predict “not fraud” too often.
Overfitting to historical patterns
Fraud strategies change over time, so the model may not detect new types of fraud.
Data quality issues
Incorrect or incomplete data can lead to poor predictions.

  Who might be unfairly affected
New users
They have little history, so the model may treat them as suspicious.
Users with unusual behavior
People who travel or make large transactions may be incorrectly flagged.
Users from certain locations
If the training data is biased, some regions may be flagged more often.
Low-income users or small businesses
Frequent small transactions may look suspicious and be unfairly blocked.

Risks of collecting this data
Privacy invasion
Collecting detailed user data may violate personal privacy.
Data breaches
If the system is hacked, sensitive financial and personal data could be exposed.
Misuse of data
Data could be used for purposes other than fraud detection without user consent.
Bias and discrimination
Certain user groups may be unfairly targeted due to biased data.
Lack of transparency
Users may not know how their data is being used or why their transactions are flagged.

While the model can effectively detect fraudulent transactions, it is not perfect and may produce errors such as false positives and false negatives.

Deploying the system without safeguards could:

Therefore, the best approach is to deploy the system with guardrails to balance effectiveness, fairness, and user trust.

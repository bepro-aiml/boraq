SCENARIO 4 — Cotton Crop Yield Prediction in Fergana Valley

Business objective:
Predict cotton yield per hectare 3 months before harvest to help with export contracts, pricing, and planning.

Target variable:
Cotton yield per hectare, measured in tons/hectare or kg/hectare.

ML problem type:
Supervised regression, because the model predicts a numerical value.

Main data sources:
1. Satellite imagery
2. Weather data
3. Soil data
4. Farm yield records
5. Irrigation data

Important input features:
1. NDVI — crop health and greenness
2. EVI — vegetation index
3. Rainfall — water availability
4. Temperature — heat stress
5. Soil moisture — plant water condition
6. Soil type — affects water retention
7. Irrigation frequency — important for cotton growth
8. Planting date — affects final yield
9. Fertilizer usage — affects crop productivity
10. Previous year yield — shows farm history

Evaluation metric:
Use RMSE and MAE.
RMSE shows large prediction errors.
MAE shows average prediction error.

Model testing:
Use temporal split and spatial split.
Temporal split: train on past years and test on a future year.
Spatial split: train on some farms and test on farms the model has never seen.

Ethical considerations:
Wrong predictions can hurt farmers financially.
Companies may offer unfair contracts if the model predicts low yield.
The model should be advisory, not fully automatic.

Human oversight:
Agronomists and farm experts should review predictions, especially when:
- prediction is very high or very low
- weather is unusual
- the farm is new
- satellite data is incomplete
- contracts or pricing decisions are affected

Data availability in Uzbekistan:
Satellite data is available.
Weather and soil data may be available.
Farm-level yield data may be hard to collect because it can be private or incomplete.

Research links:
1. NASA satellite/agriculture data: https://data.nasa.gov
2. Google Earth Engine: https://earthengine.google.com
3. Kaggle agriculture datasets: https://www.kaggle.com/datasets?tags=agriculture
4. Cotton yield prediction research: https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=cotton+yield+prediction+satellite

Personal reflection:
The hardest part was deciding what data is realistic to collect in Uzbekistan. Satellite data is easy to access, but farm-level yield data may be private or incomplete. This problem is regression because the model predicts cotton yield as a number. The model must be tested on future years and new farms. It should support human experts, not replace them.
